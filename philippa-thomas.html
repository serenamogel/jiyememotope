Who is Philippa Thomas?

Philippa Thomas is a British television newsreader and journalist who is working for BBC News as a correspondent for 25 decades. Likewise, she is the main presenter of World News Today which broadcasts on BBC World News, BBC News Channel and BBC Four.

Philippa Thomas was born on 22 November 1965 in West Yorkshire, UK but was raised up by her parents in Wakefield. She holds British citizenship and belongs to white ethnicity. Talking about her age, she is 53 years as of 2019. As far as her education, Thomas studied at Harvard University in digital media and citizen journalism.

Thomas has reported extensively from the United States, continental, South America and Africa. From 2007 to 2010, she worked as a presenter and special correspondent in the BBC Washington. Likewise, Philippa's most stories broadcasts on BBC World News, BBC America's World News America, the BBC online and PBS news broadcasts. She has worked with several television personalities like David Eades and Katty Kay.

Philippa has previously worked in many BBC shows including World News Today, BBC North America, and several others.
