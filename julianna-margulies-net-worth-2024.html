Early Life and Career Beginnings

Julianna Margulies was born on June 8, 1966, in Spring Valley, New York. She began her acting career in the early 1990s, with minor roles in films and television shows. Her breakthrough came in 1994 when she was cast as Nurse Carol Hathaway in the medical drama “ER”.

Rise to Stardom

Her role in “ER” catapulted Margulies to stardom and earned her an Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series. After leaving “ER” in 2000, she continued to act in various films and television shows, further establishing her reputation as a talented and versatile actress.

The Good Wife and Beyond

In 2009, Margulies was cast as Alicia Florrick in the legal and political drama “The Good Wife”. Her performance in the show was highly acclaimed, earning her two Primetime Emmy Awards and a Golden Globe Award. Since “The Good Wife” ended in 2016, Margulies has continued to act in various roles, further adding to her wealth.
