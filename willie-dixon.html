Biography

Willie Dixon was born in Vicksburg, MS on July 1, 1915. American blues musician and singer-songwriter who specialized in the upright bass and the guitar and who wrote “Hoochie Coochie Man.” He composed many famous pieces, many of which were performed and made popular by Muddy Waters during his time working with Chess Records. He spent time on prison farms while growing up in Mississippi, going on to sing with The Jubilee Singers.
