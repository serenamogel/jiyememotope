Shop Lizzos Eyeshadow Palette 2024

Lizzo knows how to bring the glam. The “About Damn Time” singer dropped her latest look on Instagram, and it’s stunning as always. She sported slicked-back hair that fell right below her ears, shining gold hoop earrings, and a blue, green, and purple mesh robe (think watercolor painting). However, it’s Lizzo’s glowing makeup that really stole the show here, and we know just how you can achieve it!

ColourPop Cloud 9 Pressed Powder Palette

The pop star sported a prominent black liner with a small wing, sharp brows, and a shimmering highlighter showcasing her cheekbones, the tip of the nose, and the top point of her forehead. Lizzo’s makeup artist, Alexx Mayo, revealed the exact product breakdown for fans in an Instagram post. To achieve a shiny eyelid that fades darker around the outer portion of the eye, Mayo used Colourpop’s $18 Cloud 9 Pressed Powder Palette. Per the website, the palette offers “12 silvery blues, celestial shimmers, and cool neutrals in matte, metallic, pearlescent glitter, and matte sparkle finishes.” So basically you can create an everyday chill look to a Grammy’s winning nighttime look with the amount of variation in this palette. Colourpop is well-known for its affordability, with its products being advertised as cruelty-free, long-lasting, highly blendable, and incredibly pigmented. Mayo then rounded out the makeup with Urban Decay’s 24/7 Moondust Glitter Eyeshadow Singles in the color glitter rock. It sells for $24 and offers a gorgeous pink sparkle.

If you want to recreate this look but are in desperate need of some new eyeshadow brushes—we’ve been there—then this 14-count Jessup Makeup Brush Set will do just the trick. The set consists of a foundation, contour, blush, and concealer brush, with the rest of the brushes being the perfect setup for some creative eye looks. To recreate Lizzo’s snatched eye makeup, we recommend blending the matte colors (the variety of browns in Colourpop’s palette) in the outer portion of the eye. Use the shimmering white and silver to make the inner eye pop—then show off your black eyeliner abilities with an outer wing that finishes in the inner corner. Or, just get a killer makeup artist like Lizzo. That works too!
